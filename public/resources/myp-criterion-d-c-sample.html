MYP Criterion D & C Sample Page

This sample shows how students can apply mathematics to a real-life situation and communicate their model.

Real-Life Context

A student council is planning a movie night. Tickets cost $6 each. The hall costs $180 to rent, and snacks cost $2 per student. How many students must attend for the event to make a profit?

Mathematical Model

Revenue = 6n

Cost = 180 + 2n

Profit occurs when revenue is greater than cost:

6n > 180 + 2n

4n > 180

n > 45

Interpretation and Reflection

At least 46 students must attend for the event to make a profit. The model assumes every student buys a ticket and snack costs remain fixed at $2 per student. In real life, the council should also account for unsold snacks and possible discounts.
